Pinjore Gardens

A Historic Mughal-Style Paradise

Also known as Yadavindra Gardens, Pinjore Gardens is a stunning Mughal-style garden located 22 km from Chandigarh in Pinjore, Haryana. Built in the 17th century by Mughal emperor Aurangzeb‚Äôs architect, it later underwent restoration by the Maharaja of Patiala, giving it its present grandeur.
Spread over 100 acres, the garden features beautifully terraced landscapes, fountains, pavilions, and lush greenery, offering a royal and serene atmosphere. The garden is especially enchanting during the Pinjore Heritage Festival and in the evenings when illuminated fountains create a magical ambiance.
Visitors can also explore the Japanese Garden, Mini Zoo, Nursery, and Sheesh Mahal, making it a perfect destination for family outings, photography, and leisurely walks.
